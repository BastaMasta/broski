INDIAN PENAL CODE - GENERAL OFFENCES (Simplified for Chatbots)

Section 299 – Culpable Homicide:
Whoever causes death by doing an act with the intention of causing death, or with the intention of causing such bodily injury as is likely to cause death.

Section 300 – Murder:
Culpable homicide is murder if the act is done with the intention of causing death or bodily injury sufficient to cause death.

Section 302 – Punishment for Murder:
Whoever commits murder shall be punished with death or imprisonment for life, and shall also be liable to fine.

Section 304 – Culpable Homicide Not Amounting to Murder:
Punishable with imprisonment for life, or imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

Section 307 – Attempt to Murder:
Whoever does any act with such intention or knowledge, and under such circumstances, that, if he by that act caused death, he would be guilty of murder.

Section 319 – Hurt:
Whoever causes bodily pain, disease or infirmity to any person is said to cause hurt.

Section 320 – Grievous Hurt:
Includes emasculation, permanent loss of sight/hearing, dismemberment, etc.

Section 323 – Punishment for Voluntarily Causing Hurt:
Imprisonment up to 1 year, or fine up to ₹1000, or both.

Section 324 – Causing Hurt by Dangerous Weapons:
Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.

Section 354 – Assault on Woman with Intent to Outrage Her Modesty:
Punishable with imprisonment of 1 to 5 years and fine.

Section 375 – Rape:
Defines rape including consent, force, and exceptions.

Section 376 – Punishment for Rape:
Rigorous imprisonment not less than 10 years, may extend to life, and fine.

Section 378 – Theft:
Whoever intends to take dishonestly any movable property out of the possession of any person without that person’s consent.

Section 379 – Punishment for Theft:
Imprisonment up to 3 years, or fine, or both.

Section 390 – Robbery:
Theft or extortion becomes robbery when force or fear of instant death/hurt is used.

Section 392 – Punishment for Robbery:
Imprisonment up to 10 years and fine.

Section 403 – Dishonest Misappropriation of Property:
Punishable with imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.

Section 415 – Cheating:
Deceiving any person fraudulently or dishonestly to deliver property.

Section 420 – Cheating and Dishonestly Inducing Delivery of Property:
Imprisonment up to 7 years and fine.

Section 441 – Criminal Trespass:
Entry into property with intent to commit an offence or to intimidate/annoy/insult the person in possession.

Section 447 – Punishment for Criminal Trespass:
Imprisonment up to 3 months, or fine up to ₹500, or both.

Section 499 – Defamation:
Whoever, by words, signs or visible representation, makes or publishes any imputation concerning a person, intending to harm reputation.

Section 500 – Punishment for Defamation:
Imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.

Section 504 – Intentional Insult with Intent to Provoke Breach of Peace:
Punishable with imprisonment up to 2 years, or fine, or both.

Section 506 – Criminal Intimidation:
Threatening another with injury to person, reputation or property with intent to cause alarm.

Section 509 – Word, Gesture or Act Intended to Insult the Modesty of a Woman:
Punishable with simple imprisonment up to 3 years and fine.

Section 511 – Attempt to Commit Offences Punishable with Imprisonment for Life or Other Imprisonment:
Punishment for attempting any offence punishable with imprisonment.
